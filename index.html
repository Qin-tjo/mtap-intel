Genomic landscape
Where is MTAP loss prevalent and what comes along with it?

Figure 1 · Deletion prevalence across TCGA cohorts

Figure 1 indicates that MTAP homozygous deletion (dark teal bars) is most prevalent in GBM (~43 %), MESO (~36 %), BLCA (~23 %) and PAAD (~21 %), followed by ESCA, LUSC, SKCM and HNSC (~11–18 %). Heterozygous loss (CN = 1, light teal bars) is more broadly distributed across cohorts and reaches comparable or higher per-cohort rates in some indications (e.g. SKCM ~38 %, CHOL ~36 %, LUSC ~31 %). Taylor AM 2018

Real-world cross-check. The indication rank order in Figure 1 replicates in MSK-IMPACT 50K (real-world panel-sequenced cohort, n ≈ 54k; ρrank = 0.81 across 25 matched indications). Absolute homdel rates run ~5–18× lower in MSK-IMPACT because panel-based GISTIC −2 under-detects focal homdel relative to whole-exome ABSOLUTE — this reflects a methodological difference in detection, and is independently supported by matched-sample IHC vs panel-NGS comparisons in NSCLC and mesothelioma. Taylor AM 2018 Cerami E 2012 Brune MM 2026 Febres-Aldana CA 2024

Figure 2 · Copy-number to expression fidelity

Figure 2 indicates that the deletion is generally functionally penetrant. In cohorts with enough CN = 0 samples to estimate it, MTAP median expression falls monotonically from CN = 2 → CN = 0. The per-cohort Spearman ρ between CN and expression correlates with cohort homdel % at ≈ 0.83, so the cohorts with the most homdel also tend to show the tightest CN → expression coupling. Kryukov GV 2016 Wilks C 2021

Figure 3 · Focality of the deletion

Figure 3 indicates that homozygous and heterozygous deletions at the MTAP locus have different size distributions. Homozygous deletion is predominantly focal (median ≈ 1.5–2.8 Mb, < 3 % arm-level), consistent with a driver-selected event. Heterozygous loss is mostly arm-level (median ≈ 25–115 Mb, 56–86 % arm-level), consistent with a passenger 9p loss. The MTA accumulation that creates the apparent PRMT5 dependency is generally expected to require complete focal-homozygous MTAP loss. Kryukov GV 2016

Figure 4 · Co-deletion partners

Figure 4 indicates that CDKN2A and CDKN2B are co-deleted in ≈ 88–100 % of MTAP-homdel patients in every cohort with at least 5 deleted samples. The IFN cluster (IFNA1 / B1 / W1) and ELAVL2 are reliably pulled into the deletion footprint as well. Distant tumour suppressors on other chromosomes (PTEN, RB1, TP53, NF1) appear largely untouched in MTAP-homdel patients, consistent with MTAP loss representing a focal 9p21 event. Zhang H 1996 Taylor AM 2018

Per-cohort mutational profiles of MTAP-homdel patients (e.g. PAAD ≈ KRAS-mut + TP53 + SMAD4; GBM ≈ TP53 + EGFR + PTEN; BLCA ≈ FGFR3 + TP53 + KMT2D; LUSC ≈ KMT2D + PIK3CA + NFE2L2; HNSC ≈ TP53 + CASP8 + NOTCH1; MESO ≈ low-TMB BAP1 / NF2-driven) are kept in the audit trail (03b_mutpop_long.parquet) rather than shown as a separate figure here — the indication-level signatures are reused inline in the per-indication combination strategy in Section 4. Ellrott K 2018 Chakravarty D 2017

Mechanism & rationale
Why might MTAP-deleted cancer be therapeutically actionable?
9p21 focal deletion typically removes MTAP alongside CDKN2A. Without MTAP, its substrate MTA likely accumulates and partially inhibits PRMT5, leaving MTAP-null cells dependent on residual PRMT5 activity — an apparent synthetic-lethal window for PRMT5 or upstream MAT2A inhibition.

2.1 · Mechanism flow

1
Genomic event
Focal 9p21.3 homozygous deletion (median ≈ 1.5–2.8 Mb in Figure 3) typically removes MTAP together with CDKN2A. CDKN2A is generally regarded as the selected tumour suppressor at this locus. MTAP is most often a co-deleted passenger from the cancer-cell perspective, and the therapeutic handle exploited by MTA-cooperative inhibitors. Taylor AM 2018 Zhang H 1996
2
Metabolite accumulation
MTAP appears to be the only salvage enzyme catabolising methylthioadenosine (MTA, a polyamine-biosynthesis byproduct). MTAP-null cells have been reported to accumulate MTA to high- micromolar levels — roughly 10–100× wild-type in the available models. Kryukov GV 2016 Mavrakis KJ 2016 Marjon K 2016
3
Sub-threshold PRMT5 activity
MTA structurally resembles the PRMT5 cofactor SAM and engages the same binding pocket; in vitro it has been reported to behave as a partial competitive inhibitor. Chronic MTA elevation therefore likely lowers PRMT5 catalytic output — including its symmetric dimethylation of Sm splicing factors — to a level that is generally survivable on its own but hyper-sensitive to further inhibition. Kryukov GV 2016 Marjon K 2016 Stopa N 2015 Kalev P 2021
4
Synthetic lethality
MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibitors (compounds engineered to preferentially bind the PRMT5–MTA complex), and upstream MAT2A inhibitors which lower the SAM substrate pool, appear to preferentially affect MTAP-null cells. MTAP-proficient cells retain enough PRMT5 activity to generally tolerate equivalent exposure. First-generation, MTAP-agnostic PRMT5 inhibitors do not show this selectivity in preclinical models. The synthetic-lethal window has been reported across multiple preclinical systems and is now being tested clinically. Kryukov GV 2016 Marjon K 2016 Mavrakis KJ 2016 Kalev P 2021 Engstrom LD 2023 Belmontes B 2025

2.2 · Biomarker rationale

  • Homozygous MTAP deletion is currently the most-validated biomarker.
    Detectable by standard CNA panels (FoundationOne, MSK-IMPACT). CDKN2A co-deletion appears to be a near-universal surrogate (Figure 4: ~88–100% across cohorts), but is one step removed from the addressable metabolic phenotype. Taylor AM 2018
  • Heterozygous loss likely does not qualify.
    MTA accumulation is generally thought to require complete MTAP loss; Figure 3 also indicates that heterozygous events are mostly arm-level passenger losses rather than focal driver events. Kryukov GV 2016
  • MTA itself may serve as a pharmacodynamic readout.
    MTA elevation in plasma and tissue has been reported as an enrolment biomarker and an on-treatment PD marker (especially relevant for MAT2A inhibition). Marjon K 2016 Kalev P 2021
  • MTAP loss is over-represented in oncogene-driven NSCLC.
    Aldea 2026 reports ~27–33% in ALK-rearranged, ~18–26% in RET-rearranged, and ~17–24% in EGFR-mutant NSCLC — with CDKN2A co-deletion in ~98%. These observations suggest that MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibition combined with driver-TKI may be worth testing in those subsets. Aldea M 2026

2.3 · Therapeutic strategy classes

First-gen SAM-competitive PRMT5 inhibitors
Pan-PRMT5 inhibitors that do not preferentially engage the MTAP-null context. Their therapeutic window has appeared narrow because MTAP-proficient cells are similarly inhibited at clinically achievable doses. Stopa N 2015
MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibitors
Designed to preferentially bind the PRMT5–MTA complex, so MTAP-null cells (with elevated MTA) should be selectively engaged while wild-type cells are relatively spared. Clinical-stage examples include MRTX1719 / BMS-986504 (Mirati / BMS), AMG 193 (Amgen), and TNG908 / TNG462 (Tango); first patient responses have been reported across multiple MTAP-deleted histologies in 2023–2026. Kryukov GV 2016 Marjon K 2016 Engstrom LD 2023 Belmontes B 2025 Rodon J 2024 Cottrell KM 2024 George B 2025 Rodón J 2025 Lin H 2023
MAT2A inhibitors (upstream)
Designed to lower the cellular SAM pool, likely deepening PRMT5 inhibition in MTAP-null cells. IDE397 (IDEAYA) is currently the lead clinical-stage example, with reported combination activity with sacituzumab govitecan in MTAP-deleted urothelial cancer. Marjon K 2016 Kalev P 2021 Konteatis Z 2025

2.4 · Acknowledged uncertainties

  • MTA accumulation appears to be cell-context dependent — not every MTAP-null tumour is likely to reach the same MTA threshold, and intratumoural CDKN2A / MTAP heterogeneity can probably dilute the biomarker call. Kryukov GV 2016
  • Panel-based NGS appears to under-detect MTAP loss relative to orthogonal methods. In matched-sample comparisons, MTAP IHC reports a higher MTAP-deficiency prevalence than panel NGS in NSCLC (~18% vs ~6%, Brune 2026 JTO) and is more sensitive than panel NGS for MTAP loss in pleural mesothelioma (Febres-Aldana 2024 Modern Pathology). The MSK-IMPACT vs whole-exome ABSOLUTE rate difference observed in Figure 1 (~5–18× lower in the panel cohort) is consistent with this pattern but is confounded by cohort-level differences in purity and stage, so should not be over-interpreted in isolation. Practical implication: companion-diagnostic strategies that pair panel NGS with orthogonal IHC (or FISH) are likely more robust than panel NGS alone. Brune MM 2026 Febres-Aldana CA 2024
  • Acquired resistance to MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibition has been reported in preclinical models, with resistant clones showing collateral MEK-inhibitor sensitivity. A lineage-agnostic splicing signature following PRMT5 inhibition has also been proposed as a potential pharmacodynamic biomarker. Clinical resistance evidence remains to be published. Synthesis & thesis
Taking Figures 1–4 and the Section 3 trial table together, what does the current MTAP / PRMT5 landscape suggest, and what are the most defensible next steps?

4.1 · Thesis

The MTAP / PRMT5 axis appears to have moved from a 2016 synthetic-lethality concept into a clinical platform now entering its first pivotal trials. The question is no longer whether MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibition is active in MTAP-null disease — Rodon 2024, Engstrom 2023 and Belmontes 2025 are broadly supportive of that view — but in which patient populations, with which combination partners, and with what biomarker calling, durable responses are likely. The early ~92% ORR reported for vopimetostat + daraxonrasib in MTAP-deleted RAS-mutant PDAC (Tango, June 2026; n = 12 evaluable, single-arm, non-peer-reviewed) is among the most encouraging signals released so far; if it holds up in larger and peer-reviewed reporting, it would reposition PDAC as a leading near-term opportunity for the axis. Pivotal Phase 2/3 readouts (MountainTAP-30 in PDAC; planned vopimetostat + daraxonrasib Ph3) are the next inflection point.

4.2 · Patient-selection strategy

  • Homozygous MTAP deletion is currently the most defensible enrolment biomarker; heterozygous loss likely does not qualify.
    Figure 1 indicates that MTAP homdel is most prevalent in GBM (~43%), MESO (~36%), BLCA (~23%), PAAD (~21%), ESCA (~18%), LUSC (~16%), SKCM (~14%) and HNSC (~11%). Figure 3 suggests these focal homdel events (median footprint ≈ 1.5–2.8 Mb, < 3% arm-level) are mechanistically distinct from heterozygous loss, which is predominantly arm-level passenger (median 25–115 Mb, 56–86% arm-level). The mechanistic consequence — MTA accumulation sufficient to partially inhibit PRMT5 — is generally expected to require complete MTAP loss, so heterozygous events would not normally be expected to qualify a patient.
    Figure 1 Figure 3 Kryukov GV 2016
  • Companion diagnostics for MTAP loss likely need orthogonal confirmation, not panel NGS alone.
    Two head-to-head method comparisons in matched samples support the view that panel-based NGS can under-detect MTAP loss relative to MTAP IHC and FISH. In NSCLC, Brune et al. (J Thorac Oncol 2026) reported MTAP deficiency in ~18% of cases by IHC versus the ~6% rate captured by the OPA-NGS panel they tested; the authors attributed the gap to MTAP lacking dedicated probe coverage in the panel and concluded that IHC was better suited than OPA-NGS for assessing MTAP status. In pleural mesothelioma, Febres-Aldana et al. (Modern Pathology 2024) similarly reported higher sensitivity for IHC than for panel-NGS, with FISH used as the gold standard and the discrepancies attributed mainly to low tumour purity and to the difficulty of calling focal CN events from panel read depth. Caveat — these studies report panel-vs-IHC comparisons in curated research cohorts; the cross-platform pattern observed in Figure 1 (~5–18× lower homdel rates in MSK-IMPACT panel versus whole-exome ABSOLUTE) is directionally consistent with this view but is confounded by purity / stage differences between cohorts, so does not by itself prove an enrolment-level under-detection effect. Practical implication: a tier-2 IHC (or FISH) confirmation on panel-NGS-negative cases would likely reduce the false-negative rate in MTAP-targeted trials.
    Brune MM 2026 Febres-Aldana CA 2024
  • CDKN2A is essentially always co-deleted with MTAP, but the dependency is MTAP-specific.
    Figure 4 indicates that CDKN2A is co-deleted in ~88–100% of MTAP-homdel patients across cohorts with at least 5 deleted patients, together with the focal 9p21 neighbourhood (IFN cluster, ELAVL2). The metabolic phenotype that drives PRMT5 / MAT2A dependency is specific to loss of MTAP enzymatic activity, so MTAP status should be the primary biomarker for trial enrolment. CDKN2A status remains a useful confirmatory readout where direct MTAP testing is unavailable.
    Figure 4 Zhang H 1996 Brune MM 2026
  • Oncogene-driven NSCLC subsets appear under-appreciated for MTAP enrichment.
    Aldea 2026 reports MTAP loss in ~27–33% of ALK-rearranged, ~18–26% of RET-rearranged, and ~17–24% of EGFR-mutant NSCLC — substantially higher than the pan-cohort NSCLC figures in Figure 1 (~10% LUAD, ~16% LUSC). In EGFR/ALK preclinical models the authors report that driver-TKI plus MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibition (BMS-986504) may outperform either agent alone, which — if it holds in patients — could define a distinct clinical-development lane.
    Aldea M 2026

4.5 · Key risks & open questions

  • Acquired resistance has been reported in preclinical MTAP-null models — clinical translation is unknown.
    An April 2026 bioRxiv preprint describes mechanisms of acquired resistance to MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibition in MTAP-null NSCLC, with resistant clones showing collateral MEK-inhibitor sensitivity. These observations are preclinical and not yet peer-reviewed; clinical resistance data remain to be published. Resistance-mapping work likely needs to run in parallel with Phase 3 readouts.
    [bioRxiv preprint authors — pending peer review] 2026
  • Panel-based NGS may under-detect MTAP loss in trial-enrolment populations.
    In matched-sample method comparisons, MTAP IHC has reported higher sensitivity than commercial panel NGS for MTAP loss. Brune et al. (J Thorac Oncol 2026) found MTAP deficiency by IHC in ~18 % of NSCLC samples, while the OPA-NGS panel they tested detected only ~6 %. The authors attributed the gap to limited MTAP probe coverage on the panel. Febres-Aldana et al. (Modern Pathology 2024) reported a similar IHC > panel-NGS sensitivity pattern in pleural mesothelioma, with FISH as the reference standard. Companion diagnostics for MTA-cooperative PRMT5i trials would likely benefit from orthogonal confirmation (MTAP IHC, FISH where available, MTA mass-spec for pharmacodynamic monitoring) alongside panel CN calling.
    Brune MM 2026 Febres-Aldana CA 2024
  • Several expected co-driver mutations show mutual exclusivity with MTAP homdel.
    An exploratory per-cohort Fisher analysis (held in the audit trail) indicates that TP53, RB1, and CDKN2A point-mutations are systematically mutually exclusive with MTAP-homdel. This pattern is consistent with these alterations representing alternative mechanisms for p53 / RB / cell-cycle inactivation. IDH1 mutation is mutually exclusive with MTAP-homdel in LGG; the IDH-mutant glioma branch is a genomically distinct disease from the IDH-wild-type / 9p21-deleted branch. Trial designs that assume MTAP-del × TP53-mut co-occurrence may overestimate the eligible population. The reliable co-occurrences with MTAP loss are copy-number events: CDKN2A / CDKN2B / 9p21 neighbours.
    Figure 4
  • Activity differentiation between MTA-cooperative PRMT5i and MAT2A inhibition is unresolved.
    Kalev 2021 supports a mechanistic distinction: MAT2A inhibition reduces SAM and would be expected to deepen PRMT5 suppression in MTAP-null cells. No head-to-head clinical trial currently exists. IDE397 + Trodelvy (urothelial) and BMS-986504 monotherapy have each reported response signals in their respective settings; the data are not directly comparable across these programmes because they differ in indication, in combination vs monotherapy, and in sample size and peer-review status. The question of whether MAT2A inhibition delivers a meaningfully different therapeutic window — and whether it complements or substitutes for MTA-cooperative PRMT5 inhibition — is likely to remain open until a head-to-head or sequential design is run.
    Kalev P 2021

4.6 · Worth exploring next

  • Consider a two-tier companion-diagnostic strategy: panel CN as eligibility screen, MTAP IHC as confirmatory.
    Matched-sample comparisons (Brune 2026 in NSCLC; Febres-Aldana 2024 in mesothelioma) indicate that MTAP IHC has higher sensitivity for MTAP loss than commercial panel NGS. Pairing panel CN with IHC reduces the panel-NGS false-negative rate and is resilient to MTAP probe-coverage gaps on individual commercial panels.
  • Prioritise PDAC and oncogene-driven NSCLC for new programme investment.
    Two opportunities currently stand out. (1) PDAC has the largest reported response signal to date: vopimetostat + daraxonrasib reported ~92 % ORR in 12 evaluable MTAP-del RAS-mut patients (Tango, June 2026; single-arm, non-peer-reviewed). (2) Oncogene- driven NSCLC subsets carry MTAP loss at higher rates than pan-NSCLC (Aldea 2026: ~17–33 % across ALK / RET / EGFR-rearranged disease) and pair naturally with established TKIs, defining a distinct combination-therapy lane.
